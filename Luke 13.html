Repent or Perish

Now there were some preseent at that time who told Jesus about the Galileans whose blood Pilate had mixed with their sacrifices. Jesus answered, "Do you think that these Galileans were worse sinners than all the other Galileans because they suffered this way? I tell you, no! But unless you repent, you too will all perish."

Then he told this parable: "A man had a fig tree growing in his vineyard, and he went to look for fruit on it but did not find any. So he said to the man who took care of the vineyard, 'For three years now I've been coming to look for fruit on this fig tree and haven't found any. Cut it down! Why should it use up the soil?'

"'Sir,' the man replied, 'leave it alone for one more year and I'll dig around it and fertilize it. If it bears fruit next year, fine! If not, then cut it down.'"

Jesus Heals a Crippled Woman on the Sabbath

On a Sabbath Jesus was teaching in one of the synagogues, and a woman was there who had been crippled by a spirit for eighteen years. She was bent over and could not straighten up at all. When Jesus saw her, he called fher forward and said to her, "Woman, you are set free from your infirmity." Then he put his hands on her, and immediately she straightened up and praised God.

Indignant because Jesus had healed on the Sabbath, the synagogue leader said to the people, "There are six days for work. So come and be healed on those days, not on the Sabbath."

The Lord answered him, "You hypocrites! Doesn't each of you on the Sabbath untie your ox or donkey from the stall and lead it out to give it water? Then should not this woman, a daughter of Abraham, whom Satan has kept bound for eighteen long years, be set free on the Sabbath day from what bound her?"

When he said this, all his opponents were humiliated, but the people were delighted with all the wonderful things he was doing.

The Parables of the Mustard Seed and the Yeast

Then Jesus asked, "What is the kingdom of God like? What shall I compare it to? It is like a mustard seed, which a man took and planted in his garden. It grew and became a tree, and the birds perched in its branches."

Again he asked, "What shall I compare the kingdom of God to? It is like the yeast a woman used in making bread. Even though she put only a little yeast in three measures of flour, it permeated every part of the dough."

Synopsis

Luke 13 is a collection of parables and teachings by Jesus that was recorded by Luke, one of Jesus' twelve disciples.

Reflection

The first parable about the fig tree was significant because it tells of what putting in the time and effort toward a particular goal can yield. Even though it never says the fig tree yielded fruit the extra care that the tree received shows that is never okay to give up on something without putting forth the maximum amount of effort.

What was the story of the woman about?

The comparison of the kingdom of God to the mustard seed is an interesting analogy because a mustard seed is very small, but grows into something very large. The kingdom of God is liken to this process of taking something small and growing into something large. The comparison of the yeast is similar in the same respect in which the gravity, or weight, of a small amount of something can have a dramatic effect on the outcome, or in the case the rising of bread.

In the story about the narrow door Jesus is detailing what will happen during the rapture when those who have not 'made into the house' of God try to enter into the kingdom of Heaven.
